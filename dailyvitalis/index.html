Somatic Design Project (SDP) 1

Project Topic

To design a project that engages the users’ senses and incorporate various functional electronic components into its design; we took into consideration users’ somato-sensory experience and how users engage with DailyVitalis through their sense of touch, motion, sight and hearing.

Aims & Objectives

The aim of this project was to create a medication dispensing device that houses pills and supplements. It notifies users of their medication routine in order to support habit building and consistency in their medication regimens.

Somatic design principles
Accessible & ergonomic
Supports habit building
Addresses medication non-adherence
Empowers patients to better manage their medication schedules

Critical Making Process

The steps in the design process followed the five iterative phases of critical making. It begins with the first phase of Inspiration.

Inspiration

We identified possible problems areas or pain points that we might want to address with this project and prototype build. 

Initially, we conducted a simple brainstorming conversation to get inspiration for what our prototype should be for this project. Generally, we browsed the internet for ideas or existing products that could help users achieve their goals and adhere to their New Year’s resolutions. Eventually, we ended up on the topic of taking vitamins and supplements consistently as a goal and ultimately forgetting to do so. From this conversation, we further refined our idea and ended up with the project aim being to help users to better adhere to their medication regimens.

Understanding

The understanding phase primarily involved literature searches among a few different areas, including the contributing factors towards the issue of medication non-adherence and how the prototype would fit into that solution space. Research was also conducted on the topic of habit forming, tying in psychology and conditioning practices to help build and reinforce desirable habits. In this case, we approached this problem area from a lens of empathy, and considered how we might help to remind users to take their medications more consistently.

Ideation

This phase involved brainstorming some innovative ideas for the prototype. Our team conducted a whiteboarding session to ideate possible features to incorporate, imagine some user flows and user journeys, in addition to the different somatic experience(s) that we could engage with this device.

We also solidified the list of electronic components that we planned to use in this build. Some rough sketches were done to illustrate the initial concept of the prototype and to help visualize how the components would interact with each other but also be accessible to the user.

Making

The building phase was split into a few distinct tasks:

We independently tested some electronic components for functionality but also to test any code (if needed).

The following step was very non-linear, it involved the wiring of various components to the breadboard and Arduinos. The bulk of the code was written in C++ during this next step, the code was split by major tasks between the two Arduinos used in the device. The first Arduino had code for the LCD screen and the user input from the keypad. The second Arduino had code for the remaining electronic components which included the LED bulbs, the ultrasonic sensor, passive buzzer, and the tactile button switch.

Once all the electronic components were connected together, tested for proper functionality, the shell of the device was prepared. The outer box was cut down to size, a logo for DailyVitalis was drawn out and then attached to the box.

The last stage of the building process was putting all the electronic components in place. The two circuits were independently mounted and secured in place inside the cardboard box. For the final touches: a notch was cut out of the box in order for the ultrasonic sensor to properly detect any movement, the Arduinos were connected to their power banks and the pill box casing was attached to the top of the device.

Sharing

During the “show and tell” presentation, our team shared some of the reflections on the prototype and a few ideas that we had. We over-reached with our scope outlined in the project brief, ultimately, we could not incorporate the real time clock module nor were we able to solder components together. For our next steps, we’d like to address the scope and technical limitations that we encountered and explore some possibilities for integration with Bluetooth and the Internet of Things.
